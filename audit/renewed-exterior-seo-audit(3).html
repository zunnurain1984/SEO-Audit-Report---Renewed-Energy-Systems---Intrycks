Section 1 — On-Page SEO
Homepage · Service Pages · Headings · Meta Data

Your website is invisible to Google because it speaks in the wrong language

Right now, when a homeowner in Eagan searches "roofing contractor near me," Google reads your website and struggles to understand what you do, who you serve, and where you operate. The homepage headline talks about "experiencing the difference" — but Google needs to see roofing contractor Eagan MN. Every service page says the same thing. Every heading misses an opportunity to rank. This is the most fundamental layer of SEO and the fastest to fix — no waiting for Google to crawl links or build authority. On-page changes can start showing ranking improvements within 2–4 weeks of implementation.

🔍
What's Wrong
Google can't match your pages to the searches your customers are typing. H1s, H2s, and metas contain zero target keywords.
🛠️
How We Fix It
Intrycks rewrites every heading, title tag, and meta description with precision keyword placement — one page at a time, in priority order.

🔴 Critical
Hero section overloaded with video + form — causing 5-second load time
▼

The homepage hero contains a full autoplay video and an embedded contact form above the fold. Google's Core Web Vitals require LCP (Largest Contentful Paint) under 2.5 seconds. The current homepage loads in approximately 5 seconds and weighs 3.3 MB. This directly suppresses local pack rankings — the March 2026 core update specifically tightened the link between page speed and local visibility.

Recommended Improvements
  • Replace the video in the hero with a single compressed WebP background image under 120 KB. This alone cuts LCP by 60–70%.
  • Move the video below the hero — after the first visible content block. Use the loading="lazy" attribute on the video element.
  • Remove the contact form from the hero. Relocate it below the services section or above the reviews block.
  • The hero should contain only: H1 headline → one supporting line → one CTA button. Nothing else.
  • Target homepage weight after all fixes: under 1.4 MB (currently 3.3 MB).

Homepage H1 contains zero keywords — Google cannot classify this page

The H1 currently reads: "Experience The Difference with Renewed Exterior Systems." This is a brand tagline — not a keyword signal. The H1 is the most important on-page indicator of what a page is about. Without a primary keyword here, Google has no clear signal for "roofing contractor Eagan MN" or "siding contractor Twin Cities."

Beyond the H1 issue, the heading structure throughout the homepage and service pages uses generic phrases with no keyword placement at the H2 or H3 level. 🟡 High Priority
Homepage links pointing to redirect URLs instead of final destination pages
▼

Several internal links on the homepage point to URLs that redirect to other pages rather than linking directly to the final destination. Each redirect wastes link equity that should flow to the target page, and adds a small but measurable load time cost.

Recommended Improvements
  • Crawl the site with Screaming Frog (free up to 500 URLs) or Google Search Console to identify all 3xx redirect responses on internal links
  • For each redirect link, update the href attribute to point directly to the final destination URL — bypassing the redirect entirely
  • This is a 30–60 minute fix once the redirect URLs are identified

Section 2 — Technical SEO
Speed · Code Optimisation · Images · Crawl
Intrycks Assessment
Your website is slow, heavy, and sending wrong signals to Google's crawler

At 3.3 MB and a 5-second load time, your homepage is failing Google's Core Web Vitals — a direct ranking factor since 2021, and one that became significantly more important after the March 2026 Core Update. Think of it this way: Google is like a delivery driver with limited time in your neighbourhood. If your door takes 5 seconds to open, they move on to the next house. Meanwhile, competitors with fast, clean websites are getting crawled more frequently and indexed more thoroughly. The technical fixes here are largely one-time implementation tasks — once done, they compound in benefit indefinitely. A developer can complete most of these within 1–2 weeks.

🐢
What's Wrong
A 3.3 MB homepage with render-blocking JS, uncompressed images, and unused CSS is loading twice as slowly as Google's threshold. Every second of delay costs rankings and leads.
⚡
How We Fix It
Intrycks implements JS and CSS minification, converts all images to WebP, defers non-critical scripts, and fixes the hero section. Target: sub-1.4 MB, sub-2.5s load.
🚀
What to Expect
PageSpeed score improves from failing to 80+. Google crawls more pages per visit. Local pack rankings respond to speed improvements within 3–6 weeks of the fix going live.
Timeline Week 1 — Image compression & WebP Week 1–2 — JS/CSS minification Week 3–6 — Ranking lift from speed
🔴 Critical
JavaScript bloat — needs minification and lazy loading to cut page weight
▼

The homepage loads a large JavaScript payload upfront — including code only needed when the user scrolls further down the page. This render-blocking JS delays how quickly Google and users see content, and is a direct contributor to the 5-second load time.

Three-step JS fix
  • Minify all JS files: Use WP Rocket, NitroPack, or LiteSpeed Cache — this is typically a one-click setting.
  • Defer non-critical JS: Add the defer or async attribute to scripts not needed for the initial render — Google Maps, chat widgets, form validation, and third-party analytics scripts can all be deferred.
  • Remove unused JS: Open Chrome DevTools → Coverage tab → identify JS files that are downloaded but less than 20% executed on that page. Remove or consolidate these files.
🔴 Critical
CSS unoptimised — unused CSS adding unnecessary load weight to every page
▼

The site loads CSS stylesheets containing rules for elements that don't exist on every page. This "unused CSS" adds weight to every page load with no visual benefit. Minifying and stripping unused CSS is a standard performance improvement.

Recommended Improvements
  • CSS minification: Enable via your caching plugin — WP Rocket, W3 Total Cache, and LiteSpeed Cache all include this as a setting.
  • Remove unused CSS: Use PurgeCSS or WP Rocket's "Remove Unused CSS" feature to strip styles not referenced on each specific page.
  • Critical CSS inlining: Extract only the CSS needed for above-the-fold content and inline it in the <head>. Load the remaining CSS asynchronously so it doesn't block rendering.
🔴 Critical
34 images missing alt text — invisible to Google and accessibility non-compliant
▼

34 images across the site have no alt attribute. Alt text tells Google what an image shows — reinforcing the page's topical relevance and enabling ranking in Google Image Search. It is also required for web accessibility compliance. For a contractor site, every project photo is an opportunity to reinforce keywords like "roofing Eagan MN" or "siding installation Apple Valley MN."

Alt text formula for contractor project photos
  • Roofing jobs: alt="Roof replacement completed in Eagan MN — asphalt shingle installation by Renewed Exterior Systems"
  • Siding jobs: alt="James Hardie siding installation in Apple Valley MN"
  • Team: alt="Renewed Exterior Systems roofing crew at work in Eagan Minnesota"
  • Logo: alt="Renewed Exterior Systems logo — Eagan MN roofing and siding contractor"
  • Keep alt text natural and descriptive — do not keyword-stuff
🔴 Critical
Image file sizes too large — compress all images and convert to WebP format
▼

Oversized, uncompressed images are a major contributor to the 3.3 MB homepage weight and the 5-second load time. WebP delivers the same visual quality at 40–60% smaller file sizes compared to JPG or PNG, and is natively supported by all major browsers. Google PageSpeed Insights flags uncompressed images as a direct ranking factor.

Image optimisation steps
  • Convert to WebP. On WordPress: install "WebP Express" or "Imagify" — these convert images automatically on upload and serve WebP to supported browsers.
  • Resize to actual display size. If an image displays at 800px wide on the page, the file should be 800px wide — not 2400px as exported from a camera or phone.
  • Compress before uploading. Use TinyPNG, Squoosh, or Imagify. Target sizes: hero image <150 KB, project photos <80 KB each.
  • Add loading="lazy" to all images below the fold — they load only when the user scrolls to them.
🟡 High Priority
Multiple meta descriptions on the same pages — Google ignores all of them
▼

Multiple <meta name="description"> tags are present on the same pages. When Google detects duplicate meta tags it ignores all of them and auto-generates its own snippet from page content — which is typically lower quality and less likely to earn a click. Each page must have exactly one unique meta description.

Recommended Improvements
  • The most common cause on WordPress: both the theme and an SEO plugin (Yoast, RankMath) are outputting meta descriptions simultaneously.
  • Fix: disable meta description output in the theme settings — let the SEO plugin control it exclusively.
  • Verify the fix: view-source on each page and search for name="description" — it should appear exactly once per page.
🟡 High Priority
Robots.txt is too basic — needs configuration to improve crawl efficiency
▼

The current robots.txt file is minimal. A well-configured robots.txt tells Google's crawler which pages to spend time on and which to skip — improving crawl efficiency so the most important pages (service pages, location pages, blog) get crawled and indexed faster.

Recommended robots.txt (WordPress contractor site)

✅ Pass
XML Sitemap is present — maintain and update as new pages are added
▼

The XML sitemap is present and functional — this is a pass. As new location pages and blog content are published over the coming months, the sitemap must auto-update to include them. Also confirm the sitemap is submitted inside Google Search Console.

Maintenance actions
  • Confirm sitemap URL is submitted in Google Search Console → Sitemaps section
  • Ensure your SEO plugin (Yoast / RankMath) is set to auto-update the sitemap when new pages are published
  • After each new location page is published, use GSC → URL Inspection → Request Indexing to prompt faster crawling

Section 3 — Google Business Profile & Local SEO
Location Pages · GBP Optimisation · Service Area Configuration
Intrycks Assessment
You're only competing in 1 of 4 target cities — and even Eagan isn't fully optimised

When a homeowner in Apple Valley searches "roofing contractor near me," Google's map pack shows three local businesses. Right now, Renewed Exterior Systems is almost certainly not one of them — because there are no location landing pages, the GBP service area doesn't include those cities, and the website never mentions them. The map pack is the most valuable real estate in local search: the top 3 businesses receive over 126% more traffic than organic results below them. This section is about claiming that territory across all four cities — Eagan, Apple Valley, Lakeville, and Bloomington. GBP fixes show results fastest (2–4 weeks). Location pages take 6–10 weeks to gain full traction as Google indexes and evaluates them.

What's Wrong
No location pages exist for 3 of 4 target cities. GBP likely has wrong primary category, missing service area cities, and unoptimised descriptions.

🔴 Critical
No "Areas We Serve" section on homepage or About page — missing on-site local signal
▼

Google cross-references website content against GBP data to validate service area claims. If the website never mentions Apple Valley, Lakeville, or Bloomington, Google treats those GBP service area entries with less confidence.   • Add the same section to the About page with different wording to avoid duplicate content signals
